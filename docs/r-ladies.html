Jenny Drnevich (09:38:09) (in thread): > Hi@Harshithawe are literally writing a paper about this right now and hope to submit by the end of the summer. Besides each package’s vignettes, I’d recommend working through the 3 Carpentries-style workshops we have developed:https://bioconductor.org/help/education-training/. The first, bioc-intro, is a general introduction to R. The second, bioc-rnaseq, is intermediate and focused on analysis of RNA-Seq data. The third, bioc-project, is an introduction to Bioconductor specifically, it’s history, the website layout, how to find packages you need, and some of the common S4 data structures used acrossed Bioc packages. - Attachment (bioconductor.org): Bioconductor > The Bioconductor project aims to develop and share open source software for precise and repeatable analysis of biological data. We foster an inclusive and collaborative community of developers and data scientists.
